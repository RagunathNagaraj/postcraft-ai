There's a pattern playing out across Dallas right now that's costing first-time buyers their dream homes. It has nothing to do with their budget. It's timing.

The Order Most Buyers Get Wrong

Most first-time buyers start house hunting first. They scroll listings, book showings, fall in love with a place, and only then start thinking about financing. By the time they call a lender, the home they wanted is already under contract.

Why Timing Matters More in Dallas

Dallas continues to draw buyers relocating for work. That demand means well-priced homes don't sit long. A buyer without a pre-approval letter is starting several steps behind anyone who already has one.

What Pre-Approval Actually Does for You

Pre-approval tells you your real budget before you start touring homes. It also speeds up the closing process once your offer is accepted.
